What is Japanese Martial Arts?

A martial art is a style or school primarily taught in combat techniques, with bare hands or with a weapon. Historically, this learning incorporates a spiritual and moral dimension aimed at self-control and is enriched by multiple knowledge. Thus, martial arts aim at the overall development of the individual: external (strength, flexibility), internal (energy, health), intellectual and moral.

Ninjutus

Ninjutsu, a martial art developed in feudal Japan by the legendary Ninja, is characterized by defense and attack techniques considered devastating. Often used for espionage, these techniques are trickery and deception. Secret residences for many centuries and transmitted within real clans, through a rigorous training both physical and mental.

Kenjutsu

Kenjutsu is the art of the samurai sword. It belongs to the ancient Japanese martial arts and falls into the category of bujutsu, the warrior techniques of feudal Japan.The training is done in pairs with a bokken or a shoto. At the highest level, it is performed with a katana or a wakizashi.
